Core Values

  • Quality: Always serve fresh, hot food prepared to high standards. Ensure every order meets customer expectations and upholds Wendy's reputation.
  • Integrity: Treat customers and team members with honesty and respect. Always prioritize ethical decision-making in every situation.
  • Customer Service: Go above and beyond to create a memorable dining experience. Listen actively to feedback and resolve issues effectively.
  • Teamwork: Foster collaboration and support among team members. Celebrate successes and work together to address challenges.

Appearance and Uniform

Maintain a clean, professional appearance with the Wendy’s uniform, which includes a clean shirt, hat, and name tag. Non-slip shoes are required for safety. Long hair must be tied back, and avoid wearing strong perfumes or excessive jewelry. Personal hygiene is essential, so ensure you are always presentable to customers.

Basic Responsibilities

Food Prep and Safety

  • Follow food preparation guidelines meticulously to ensure consistency and safety.
  • Wash hands regularly, especially after breaks, handling non-food items, or using the restroom.
  • Wear gloves when handling food and replace them as needed to prevent contamination.
  • Store food items at appropriate temperatures and label them correctly with preparation and expiration times.

Customer Service

  • Greet every customer warmly with a smile and maintain a friendly attitude.
  • Repeat orders back to customers to ensure accuracy and clarity.
  • Address special requests with attention to detail, and ensure every guest leaves satisfied.
  • Handle complaints calmly and involve a manager when necessary for resolution.

Cleaning

  • Ensure all workstations remain clean and organized throughout your shift.
  • Regularly sanitize surfaces, tools, and equipment in line with health department standards.
  • Empty trash bins promptly and separate recyclables properly.
  • Complete assigned deep-cleaning tasks as scheduled by management.

Scheduling and Attendance

Being on time is essential to smooth operations. Use the scheduling app to track your shifts, request changes, or pick up additional hours. Notify your manager at least 24 hours in advance for planned absences, and as early as possible in case of emergencies. Keep track of your breaks to comply with labor regulations and avoid overworking yourself.

Handling Emergencies

  • Health Emergencies: Report any injuries or illnesses immediately. For serious cases, follow the emergency protocol and call for medical help if needed.
  • Equipment Malfunctions: Shut off faulty equipment and inform a manager right away. Never attempt repairs unless authorized and trained to do so.
  • Customer Incidents: Stay calm and professional. Contact a manager to handle disputes, accidents, or any other emergencies involving customers.

Tips for Success

  • Take initiative: Look for ways to help your teammates or improve operations when tasks are completed.
  • Communicate effectively: Share ideas, concerns, and feedback openly with your team and managers.
  • Maintain a positive attitude: Your energy sets the tone for the team and customers.
  • Be detail-oriented: Small actions, like properly wrapping food or ensuring tables are spotless, make a big difference.
  • Embrace growth: Seek opportunities to learn new skills and advance in your role.

Thank you for being part of our team! Let’s make every day a success at Wendy’s!
